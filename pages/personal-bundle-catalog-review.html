Product review · Personal bundle catalog

One person. Many bundles. One reliable way back in.

AgentState already gives each project and private workspace its own durable memory. The personal catalog adds the missing orientation layer: a simple place for you and your agents to find the right bundle without merging or centralizing the knowledge inside it.

Designed because juggling multiple bundles is now a real workflow
The product gap

Independent bundles are a strength—until you lose your place

Project boundaries keep knowledge understandable, portable, and private. But as the number of bundles grows, remembering where each one lives becomes its own job.

Today

Navigation lives in your head

You remember paths, switch repositories, reopen launchers, and explain to an agent which workspace you meant. The bundles work; orientation does not.

With the catalog

Name the place you want

“Personal,” “AgentState,” or “client work” becomes enough context to locate the bundle. Once selected, the normal AgentState experience takes over.

User journey

A small layer that makes many workspaces feel coherent

Register

Name the bundles you actually use. Nothing scans your machine or publishes a private workspace.

Orient

See the known places you work and whether each one is currently available on this machine.

Open

Choose a bundle and enter its existing Page launcher, with its own domain model, records, and views intact.

Trust

The catalog remembers where things are; it never absorbs, merges, or silently reroutes the underlying knowledge.

Product values

What this should preserve

Local by default

Your catalog can contain private names and locations. It stays on your machine unless you deliberately choose otherwise.

Clear authority

The catalog chooses a destination. The selected bundle remains the only authority for its knowledge.

CLI-first portability

A normal npm installation should be enough. Skills may teach the workflow, but they are not required to make it work.

Honest limits

The first release solves finding and opening bundles. It does not pretend to be a universal cross-project dashboard.

First product slice

Useful immediately, without betting on the whole future

What ships first

  • Add, list, rename, and remove known bundles
  • Let an agent resolve one named bundle safely
  • Open the selected bundle's existing UI
  • Work from any directory and without a skill
  • Keep the registry private and explicit

What waits for evidence

  • One dashboard aggregating records from every bundle
  • Cross-bundle writes or transactions
  • Automatic filesystem discovery
  • Hosted accounts, subscriptions, or remote reopening
  • Portable identity across several machines
